About the Center

About Mine Rescue Center

A free, community-maintained home for mine rescue training material and competition records.

Our Mission

The Mine Rescue Center will be a resource for mine rescue professionals across the world. While this is a major undertaking, it'll start small and grow into something of value to others.

Initially, we're focusing on the U.S. mine rescue scene and MSHA's rules and competitions. Our goal is to create something that can be run sustainably, with high quality, and useful to mine rescue professionals.

What You'll Find Here

  • Past Problems — a repository of old competition problems, recovered from miningquiz.com and collected from competition organizers.
  • Competition Calendar — all mine rescue competitions with locations, event details, and (starting in 2026) results and problems after each competition.

History

Mine Rescue Center was started in 2026 by Jacob Olson, a mining engineering student at the Colorado School of Mines and a member of the Colorado School of Mines Mine Rescue Team (CSMMRT).

It grew out of a gap the team kept running into: finding mine rescue competition problems to train on, and getting good training material in general. The Center is an effort to close that gap — for CSMMRT and for every team that needs it.
